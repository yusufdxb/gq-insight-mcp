[00:34] Interviewer: You paused a company-wide rollout. What stopped it?
[00:45] P-8344: SSO. We mandate SAML single sign-on for anything that touches employee data, and Northwind's SSO is gated behind the enterprise tier at a price jump we couldn't justify for a tool we were still piloting.
[03:02] P-8344: Security can't be a paywall for us. In healthcare, SSO and audit logs aren't premium features, they're the cost of entry. The moment SSO is "talk to sales," I have to assume the security posture is an afterthought.
[05:30] Interviewer: Beyond SSO, any other blockers?
[05:38] P-8344: Audit logging was thin. I need to know who approved what and when, exportable, immutable. What's there is a basic activity feed, not an audit trail I could hand a compliance officer.
[07:55] P-8344: And data residency. I asked where the data lives and got a vague answer. For us that's a hard gate, not a nice-to-have.
[10:12] Interviewer: What would unblock the rollout tomorrow?
[10:20] P-8344: SSO in the tier we're already on, a real exportable audit log, and a straight answer on data residency. Give me those three and I sign for all 900 seats this quarter.
